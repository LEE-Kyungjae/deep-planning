## Question

What should Palamedes do next when no external test project exists, without
turning an interesting philosophy into an untestable identity?

## Three independent readings

### 1. Genealogist

Palamedes's distinctive object is not the generated answer. It is the trace of
how a view became possible: which prior view existed, what encounter disturbed
it, which relation changed, what action followed, and what survived contact
with reality.

This explains why ordinary plans and chat transcripts are insufficient. Plans
overwrite; transcripts preserve too much without distinguishing a question,
hypothesis, decision, and commitment.

Its danger is turning every change into a meaningful lineage after the fact.

### 2. Falsifier

Most of Palamedes's current value may be reproducible with disciplined Markdown,
Git history, and a strong general-purpose agent. More schema can create
administrative ceremony, and self-application can reward records merely because
Palamedes is designed to record them.

The strongest falsification is therefore not “did it produce an interesting
insight?” It is:

> Did a pre-registered record let a later reviewer recover an actual change,
> contradiction, or decision that ordinary notes did not—and did that recovery
> alter the next step?

If not, the inquiry layer is storage theater.

### 3. Operator

Waiting for a perfect external project makes the first probe dependent on an
unavailable input. Palamedes itself is already a live project with genuine
design uncertainty, implementation choices, and an owner who can reject the
agent's interpretation.

Self-application is usable as case zero, not as product proof. It can validate
the protocol and expose missing fields. It cannot establish comparative value
or generality.

## Synthesis

The next product object should be treated as a **decision-lineage experiment**,
not yet as Palamedes's new identity.

A useful lineage has six linked events:

```text
prior view
  -> prediction
  -> bounded intervention
  -> observation (including surprise)
  -> view delta and invariant
  -> next decision
```

The present `view_transitions` structure covers much of this, but “what remained
unchanged” and an explicit pre-step prediction are not consistently first-class.
Without them, normal continuity can be rewritten as a pivot and outcomes can be
matched to vague expectations.

## Concrete result

Palamedes becomes its own **case zero** for five bounded cycles. This is a
protocol-debugging run, not one of the three external cases in the existing
success metric.

For each cycle:

1. Freeze the prior view, predicted learning, and next small step before acting.
2. Keep an ordinary Markdown note in parallel.
3. After the step, record observation, surprise, view delta, and invariant.
4. Have the owner mark interpretation errors or overclaims.
5. At cycle five, create a blinded reconstruction packet from the Palamedes
   record and the ordinary note.

The run succeeds only if:

- all five cycles contain pre-action predictions;
- at least one owner correction is preserved rather than smoothed away;
- a blinded reviewer can recover the trigger and invariant of a transition
  more accurately from one record;
- at least one recovered difference changes a subsequent probe or decision.

## What is deliberately not concluded

- Palamedes improves startup success.
- View-transition lineage is the final product identity.
- More references automatically improve creativity.
- A multi-agent debate is required.
- Case zero can replace external evaluation.

## Newly visible risk

The current plan contains two different evaluation targets:

- static strategy preference and stop/pivot/positioning decisions;
- longitudinal fidelity and information-producing next steps.

Collapsing them into one score would hide failure. They should remain separate
tracks until evidence shows a causal relation between them.
